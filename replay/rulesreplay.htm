If you're familiar with the TV version of Jeopardy then you're mostly familiar with these rules. We'll be doing a few things differently; most notably we are doing away with the "phrasing the answer in the form of a question" part. The questions and answers are going to be phrased normally.

Game Play

The game is played in two rounds (Single Jeopardy and Double Jeopardy), plus Final Jeopardy. In each of the first two rounds the board is filled with six categories, each with five questions of increasing difficulty. The questions are given an ascending point value (from 100 to 500 in Single Jeopardy and from 200 to 1000 in Double Jeopardy). Before the game, the contestants will be put in a random play order, and in that order will take turns answering questions. The question is selected by whomever has "control of the board." Board control is given to a random contestant to start the game, and passes to a player if he answers a question correctly. So it might be Dirtrum's turn, but if CTS-1 was the last to correctly answer a question it will be CTS-1 selecting the question for Dirtrum to answer. Though players know what the categories are, they don't know what questions are behind what point values: they are selecting a category and a point value.

Each contestant will have a time limit. No response to a question within the time limit is considered an incorrect answer.

Daily Doubles

Hidden on the board are Daily Doubles. There is one Daily Double in the Single Jeopardy round, and two in the Double Jeopardy round. If a player is presented with a Daily Double, instead of the chosen point value he gets an opportunity to wager a point amount, up to the total number of points he currently has. But in all cases, no matter how many points he has (even if he has a negative point total), he'll get the opportunity to wager at least 100 points for the Single Jeopardy Daily Double or 200 points for a Double Jeopardy Daily Double.

Scoring

In the first two rounds, answering a question correctly gives the contestant the point value of the question. Answering the question incorrectly incurs a 100-point deduction from his total in Single Jeopardy, and a 200-point deduction in Double Jeopardy.

If the player is putting a wager on a Daily Double, he is answering to win or lose the amount of his wager.

Final Jeopardy

After the conclusion of Single Jeopardy and Double Jeopardy comes Final Jeopardy, which can only be contested among any contestants that have positive point totals through the first two rounds. Final Jeopardy is an opportunity for all the remaining players to wager any amount on one question that they will all attempt to answer.

First, the Final Jeopardy category is revealed. Knowing the category, each player submits a wager in secret up to a maximum of his current point total. Then the question is revealed, and each player has a short amount of time to email their answer to the host. One by one, the players' wagers and answers are revealed, and their total points adjusted up or down by the amount of the wagers.

The winner is the player with the highest point total. In the event of a tie, the winner is the one that scored the most points in Final Jeopardy. If a second tiebreaker is needed, the player with the most correct answers will be the winner.
